Simple Yam Porridge Recipe

Yam porridge, also known as asaro, is a classic Nigerian comfort food. It is soft, rich, and slightly oily from palm oil, with a thick texture created by mashed yam.

Recipe Overview

Time

  • Prep time: 10-15 minutes
  • Cooking time: 20-25 minutes
  • Total time: 35-40 minutes

Serving

  • Serves 2-3 people.
  • Serve hot.

Difficulty

  • Easy and beginner-friendly.

Ingredients

  • Yam, half tuber
  • Palm oil
  • 2-3 fresh tomatoes, or blended tomatoes
  • 1 small onion
  • Pepper, to taste
  • Seasoning cube and salt
  • Crayfish, optional
  • Fish, meat, or egg, optional
  • Water

Instructions

  1. Peel and cut the yam into small cubes.
  2. Rinse the yam well.
  3. Put the yam in a pot.
  4. Add enough water to just cover it.
  5. Add onions, tomatoes, pepper, and a little salt.
  6. Cook for 10-15 minutes.
  7. Add palm oil, crayfish, and seasoning.
  8. Add fish or meat if using.
  9. When the yam is soft, mash some pieces with a spoon.
  10. Leave some chunks for texture.
  11. Let it cook for another 5-10 minutes until thick.
  12. Taste and adjust the seasoning.

Tips

  • Do not add too much water because the porridge should be thick.
  • Stir often so it does not burn.
  • Palm oil gives it an authentic Nigerian taste.
  • Add scent leaves or spinach at the end for extra flavour.

Note: Always use fresh ingredients for the best taste.
